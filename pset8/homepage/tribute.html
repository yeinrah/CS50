Hedy Lamarr

An Ambitious Inventor

Hedy Lamarr was an Austrian-American actress, inventor, and film producer. She was part of 30 films in her 28 years of acting career, and co-invented an early version of frequency-hopping spread spectrum(FHSS) which paved the way for the invention of Wi-Fi.

Timeline

  • 1914 - born Hedwig Eva Maria Kiesler on November 9, in Vienna, Austria-Hungary
  • 1930 - started her acting career by gaining her first role as an extra for "Money on the Street"
  • 1937 - contracted with MGM studio and moved to America, changed her surname to Lamarr
  • 1938 - became famous after her success for performing in "Algiers"
  • 1942 - invented frequency hopping spread device with her friend George Antheil, and was granted a patent
  • 1945 - founded her own production company "Mars Film Corporation"
  • 1949 - performed in "Samson and Delilah" which became her greatest movie career
  • 1960 - was honored with a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame
  • 1997 - honored with the Electronic Frontier Foundation's Pioneer Award & became the first woman to receive the Invention Convention's BliBIE Gnass Spirit of Achievement Award
  • 2000 - died on January 19, 2000, in Casselberry, Florida, aged 85
  • 2014 - After her death, Hedy Lamarr was inducted into the National Inventors Hall of Fame
